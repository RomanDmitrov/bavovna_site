Bavovna Event ("we", "us", "our") organises parties, concerts, weddings and corporate events in Dublin, Ireland. This Privacy Policy explains what personal information we collect through this website, why we collect it, and the choices you have. We are committed to handling your data lawfully, fairly and transparently under the EU General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR) and the Irish Data Protection Act 2018.

1. Who we are

Bavovna Event is an events organiser based in Dublin. For any privacy question you can reach us at bavovna.event.ireland@gmail.com. We act as the data controller for the information you submit through this site.

2. What we collect

We only collect what you choose to give us. When you complete our booking or partnership forms, that typically includes:

  • your name or company name;
  • contact details such as email address, phone number or Telegram handle;
  • event details you share with us — event type, approximate number of guests, budget range and the message you write;
  • basic technical information your browser sends automatically, such as approximate region and device type.

We do not ask for, and please do not send us, special-category data (for example health, religious or political information) or payment card numbers through these forms.

3. Why we use it and our legal basis

  • To respond to your enquiry and prepare a proposal for your event — this is necessary to take steps at your request before entering into a contract.
  • To organise and deliver an event you book with us — performance of our contract with you.
  • To keep records and improve our service — our legitimate interest in running and developing the business, balanced against your rights.
  • To send occasional updates about our events, only where you have asked us to — your consent, which you may withdraw at any time.

4. Sharing your information

We never sell your data. We share it only where it is needed to answer you or run your event — for example with a specific venue, contractor or service provider working on that event, and with the service that delivers our form notifications by email. These providers may process data outside Ireland; where they do, we rely on appropriate safeguards such as the European Commission's Standard Contractual Clauses. We may also disclose information where the law requires it.

5. How long we keep it

Enquiries that do not lead to a booking are kept for up to 24 months so we can follow up, then deleted. Information relating to events we deliver is kept for as long as needed to fulfil the booking and to meet legal and accounting obligations, after which it is securely erased.

6. Your rights

Under the GDPR you have the right to access, correct, erase, restrict or object to the processing of your personal data, and the right to data portability. You can exercise these rights — or ask a question — by emailing us. See our GDPR Data Request Policy for how requests are handled. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Irish Data Protection Commission (dataprotection.ie).

7. Cookies

This site uses only the minimal storage needed to remember your language and light/dark theme choice on your own device. We do not use advertising or cross-site tracking cookies.

8. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. The date at the top shows when it was last revised; significant changes will be reflected here.
